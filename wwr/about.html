History

White water rafting has a rich history that dates back thousands of years. Indigenous peoples in various parts of the world, including South America and Asia, used rafts made from natural materials to navigate rivers for transportation, fishing, and trade. The modern sport of white water rafting began to take shape in the mid-20th century, particularly in the United States. In the 1950s and 1960s, adventurers and outdoor enthusiasts started exploring rivers with inflatable rafts, leading to the development of specialized equipment and techniques. The sport gained popularity in the 1970s and 1980s, with the establishment of commercial rafting companies offering guided trips on famous rivers like the Colorado River through the Grand Canyon. Today, white water rafting is enjoyed worldwide, attracting thrill-seekers and nature lovers alike to experience the excitement of navigating rapids while appreciating the beauty of river ecosystems.
